<p><br>Cholesterol is like a gossamer thread connecting triglycerides to cardiovascular diseases. It is an organic compound produced by the liver and found in varying levels throughout our bodies. An imbalance of cholesterol can cause high triglyceride levels, raising the risk for heart-related issues such as coronary artery disease and even stroke. A higher level of LDL (bad) cholesterol increases the risk, while HDL (good) cholesterol helps reduce it. High blood pressure also contributes to elevated triglyceride levels, making controlling both factors key in managing overall health and wellbeing.</p>
