Hemp oil and CBD (copy)

Ica March 31, 2023

Hemp oil and CBD oil are both derived from the same plant, Cannabis Sativa. However, there is a difference between the two products that should be considered when making a choice for personal health needs. Hemp oil has been used in many forms throughout history due to its high nutritional value and potential therapeutic benefits, while CBD oil has recently become increasingly popular due to its purported health effects.

Hemp oil is made from pressing hemp seeds, which contain little to no THC (the psychoactive compound found in marijuana). It can be used as a dietary supplement and may provide essential fatty acids, protein, vitamins and minerals. The most common uses of hemp oil include cooking or baking with it, adding it to smoothies or salad dressings, and using it as an ingredient in body care products such as lotions and soaps.

CBD oil on the other hand is extracted from cannabis flowers containing higher levels of cannabinoids such as THC and cannabidiol (CBD). This type of product typically requires more processing than hemp seed-based oils because it contains multiple compounds rather than just one particular nutrient. Depending on how it’s processed, CBD oil can have varying concentrations of different substances including terpenes, flavonoids and other active ingredients. Generally speaking it’s thought to possess greater medicinal properties than hemp seed-based oils but further research will be needed before this claim can be verified conclusively.
